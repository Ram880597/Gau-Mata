Promoting Desi Cow Welfare and Sustainable Products

Desi Cow Mata

The Maharashtra government recently declared indigenous cows as "Rajya Mata-Gomata," recognizing their historical, nutritional, and agricultural significance. This decision highlights the importance of desi cows in India's cultural and spiritual heritage, as well as their role in sustainable farming practices. Desi cow milk is valued for its nutritional benefits and is often used in Ayurvedic treatments. Additionally, cow dung plays a crucial role in organic farming by enhancing soil fertility. To support the care of these cows, the government has introduced a subsidy scheme, offering ₹50 per day for each cow in goshalas (cow shelters). This initiative aims to preserve indigenous breeds, whose population has been declining.
